🔑 Your lineup is better than the batting averages suggest
At 9U, a lot of runs score on walks rather than hits. Look at your OBP column: Natalie D (.632), Summer S (.603), Murphy D (.531), Chloe S (.512), Brooklyn S (.457). Five players getting on base at .450+. At 10U where pitchers throw more strikes, the players with the real swing fundamentals will separate. Natalie D and Summer S are your franchise pieces.
🔑 Pitching — K rate is the only number that matters right now
Ignore all the ERAs. Here's what to pay attention to: Brooklyn S has 101 Ks in 60 IP (15.1 K/9). Chloe S has 63 Ks in 52 IP (10.9 K/9). If Finley G and Harper S are coming with the team to 10U, they showed 19.0 and 18.7 K/9 respectively in small samples. Any pitcher with a K/9 above 12 at this age has real arm stuff that coaching can develop into an effective 10U arm. Focus your bullpen sessions on walk reduction — the strikeout ability is already there.
🔑 Two players need contact priority attention
Ellie K (.047 BA, 81% K rate) and Piper S (.073 BA) are your two contact-development priorities. Both draw walks, which means they understand the strike zone — that's the hardest thing to teach and they already have it. The issue is purely mechanical contact. Get them on the tee 3x per week and in soft-toss. Both players have the baseball IQ to improve quickly.
🔑 Murphy D is a hidden gem
She hits .321 but gets on base at .531. That .210 gap between BA and OBP is enormous — it means she walks constantly (25 BB in 56 AB). She's your ideal leadoff hitter in a world where 10U pitchers still struggle to throw strikes. Her only weakness is low RBI (4) — she's getting on base but not driving the ball. Work on her to use the whole field and hit grounders through the gaps. She could develop into a table-setter and genuine threat.
