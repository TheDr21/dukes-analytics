Team Philosophy & Goals

We are committed to competing at the highest level appropriate for our team, balancing development with meaningful competitive challenges. Our target is to maintain a 50–70% win rate while consistently pushing players beyond their comfort zones. Our tournament plan reflects this approach, with a primary focus on 11U A/B competition, selective participation in 12U C events, and a commitment to at least two premier tournaments in Spring 2027.

Fundamentals First
We prioritize strong fundamentals — throwing, catching, hitting mechanics, fielding, and baserunning — as the foundation for long-term success.
Game IQ & Ownership
We develop players' understanding of the game through situational awareness, decision-making, and leadership. Players are encouraged to take ownership; for example, catchers will call pitches and learn to manage game strategy and sequencing.
Mental Performance
We emphasize a growth mindset, focus under pressure, confidence, and resilience. Effort and attitude are non-negotiable; results will follow.
Team-First Culture
We foster accountability and a unified team environment centered on effort, attitude, and support. Parents play a critical role in reinforcing this by supporting the team as one group.
Competitive Progression
We intentionally challenge players through higher-level competition and purposeful development to prepare them for the next stage of their growth.
📈
Player Development

Our development approach centers on a team-wide emphasis on hitting approach and overall game strategy, ensuring every player builds a strong understanding of situational play. Specialty skill development — including pitchers, catchers, and all position players — is addressed through small group and individualized sessions led by Steve LaFever to provide focused, high-impact instruction. Pitcher workload management is a priority: building depth while targeting approximately six innings per day or twelve innings per tournament. Targeted individual development areas are identified and refined throughout practices to support each athlete's growth.
