FIP vs ERA — luck-adjusted pitching

FIP (Fielding Independent Pitching) measures only what pitchers control — strikeouts, walks, and home runs — removing defense and luck. Lower is better.

Glomb: FIP 2.70, ERA 4.24 (+1.54 gap) — she's been significantly unluckier than her ERA shows. Her underlying command is elite. The runs allowed are not entirely her fault.

Porter: FIP 3.18, ERA 3.09 (–0.09) — ERA and FIP align perfectly. What you see is what you get.

Rocco: FIP 5.74, ERA 4.30 (–1.44) — she's been luckier than her walk rate deserves. The control (52% strikes, 3.22 BB/INN) is unsustainable — expect ERA regression.

Dantry: FIP 3.97, ERA 4.63 (+0.66) — like Glomb, slightly unluckier than skill suggests. But the late-season ERA spike is a real trend.

Run environment — how the team scores

Bimodal scoring: The team either scores 0–1 runs (19 games) or blows the doors off (14 games with 10+ runs). There's very little middleground — the 4–5 run game (14 games) is the sweet spot that flips close losses.

Close-game problem: In 1-run games the team is 5-10. In 2-run games it's 4-7. That's a combined 9-17 in games decided by 1 or 2 runs — a major area of opportunity. Winning even half of those close losses would push the record to roughly 50-21-3.

Left on base (LOB) — strand rate issues

Tortorice leads in LOB (96) — she gets on base (17 BB) but the team consistently strands her. That's partly lineup construction and partly her own RISP struggles (.194 BA with runners in scoring position).

Dantry: 9 2-out RBI in 59 LOB — decent 2-out production but 59 LOB over the season suggests she's often stranded too. Her RISP (.413) is solid though, suggesting teammates aren't driving her in.

Chimino: only 1 two-out RBI all season — lowest on the team. Her .121 BA/RISP is the lowest on the team by a significant margin.
