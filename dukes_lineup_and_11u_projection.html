11U season projection

The rule changes that matter

Mound moves back (~35ft → ~40ft)
More distance means more time for hitters to read. Batters who struggle with proximity-based velocity (Marple, Schmidt) may improve. Pitchers lose the proximity edge — command becomes the primary differentiator. Porter and Glomb are groundball pitchers who don't rely on proximity, so they adapt best.
Ball gets bigger (11-inch → 12-inch)
Harder to grip and control. Pitchers with marginal command (Rocco, late-season Dantry) will feel this most early in the year. Contact hitters like Porter and Glomb adapt fastest because mechanics don't change — just the timing window.
Bases extend to 60ft (from 55ft)
Slightly harder to steal. LaFever (78 SB) and Glomb (73 SB) have the speed to absorb it. Catchers LaFever and McWilliams may actually see CS% improve as throws become proportionally similar to what older levels demand.
Competition rises significantly
11U is where physical development gaps widen. Better opposing pitching likely drops team RS from 5.5 to ~4.2 per game. Teams with strong pitching development will be harder to score on — making the Lady Dukes' pitching depth critical.

Predicted offensive output at 11U (relative to 10U)

Bottom line: Porter, Glomb, LaFever, and Dantry carry over nearly intact. The run production gap between them and the rest will widen at 11U. The team's biggest lever is pitching: if Glomb and Porter develop a reliable second pitch, and Rocco hardens her strike%, this team competes for a tournament title. If pitching stagnates, .500 is the ceiling.
